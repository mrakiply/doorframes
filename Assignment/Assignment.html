What makes a Doorframe?

Here are some defining characteristics of a doorframe.

The Sill

The sill is one of the vital features of a doorframe. One of its features is that it is the base of the doorframe and prevents the rest of the doorframe from bending or collapsing. Furthermore, when installed properly, it can prevent the entry of dust, insects and the likes into your house.

The Threshold

The threshold sits right on top of the sill of the doorframe. This part of the doorframe helps to reduce the gap between the door and the floor. It also further enhances the benefits of the sill by also helping to prevent water from seeping into the house.

The Transom

It is an optional component of the traditional doorframe that is usually situated over the rest of the doorframe. It is usually used to enhance the look of the doorframe and let light into the house. When properly utilised, it can truly make your door look amazing and can really add to the aesthetic of your house.
